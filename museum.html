Featured Highlight

The Chronos Amulet

Discovered in the ruins of a 17th-century Parisian workshop, the Chronos Amulet is said to have belonged to the legendary illusionist 'Cagliostro'. Its inner mechanisms defy contemporary physics, featuring gears that turn even when isolated from gravity.

Origin Paris, 1684
Material Meteoric Iron

Current Exhibits

Nine chambers of wonder, thousands of stories.

Chamber I

The Golden Age

Featuring original escape gear used by Harry Houdini during his legendary 1912 world tour. See the 'Water Torture Cell' keys and the 'Handcuff King's' personal ledger.

Chamber II

Arcane Artifacts

Step back into the 16th century. Our collection includes original alchemical tomes, medieval sleight-of-hand tools, and the mysterious 'Oracle Bowl' of Delphi.

Chamber III

Mechanical Magic

Discover the clockwork wonders of the 18th century. Witness the 'Chess-Playing Turk' and the self-writing 'Boy Automaton' in a rare live demonstration.

Chamber IV

The Future

From digital misdirection to aerospace-grade levitation rigs. See the evolution of magic through the lens of modern tech.

Chamber V

Psychology Lab

Explore the lab where misdirection is mapped. See the cognitive research that fuels today's greatest mentalists.

Chamber VI

Cardistry Vault

A tribute to the art of the flourish. Discover the history of playing cards and the rare decks that define the craft.

Chamber VII

The Forge

See where the impossible is built. A reconstruction of Alexander Thorne's original workshop and tools.

Chamber VIII

Hidden Library

Thousands of rare tomes and secret manuscripts. The world's most complete collection of magic literature.

Chamber IX

The Stage

Witness the massive stage props that defined 20th-century spectacles. From levitating cars to disappearing tigers.

1895

The Foundation

Alexander Thorne opens the first 'Arcane Workshop' in London, focusing on brass clockwork illusions.

The War Secrets

Arcane contributes misdirection techniques to the Allied forces, evolving psychological magic.

1988

Las Vegas Debut

The Museum moves to its current home in Nevada, becoming the world's largest repository of magic.
